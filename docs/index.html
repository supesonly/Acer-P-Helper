Why choose Predator Control?

A native utility designed to give you direct command without the overhead.

Toggle through Silent, Balanced, Performance, Turbo, and Eco modes. Auto-switches limits instantly depending on whether you're plugged in or on battery power.

Take charge of your cooling with Auto, Max, or Custom settings. Perfect for keeping temperatures low under heavy loads or dead silent during productivity.

Easily switch between 60Hz to extend battery life on the go, and your panel's maximum refresh rate (up to 240Hz/360Hz) for gaming.

Complete control over keyboard backlight styles: Static, Breathing, Neon, Wave, Shifting, Zoom, Meteor, and Twinkling, with precise brightness/speed adjustments.

Saves your custom configs in the Windows Registry so they load automatically on startup and persist cleanly across system reboots.

Minimizes to the system tray on startup using a simple `-hidden` launch parameter. Change presets directly from the tray icon menu with a right click.

Replacing PredatorSense

Since the app utilizes Acer's WMI interface directly, you can safely disable bloated OEM background services.

01

Disable PredatorSense Services

Open Services (Win + R → type services.msc → press Enter) and set these services to Disabled to stop background resource hogging:

  • Acer Gaming Service — Main daemon
  • Acer Quick Access Service — OSD utilities
  • AcerService — Core update manager
  • Acer Power Button Service — Button polling
02

Remove from Startup

Open Task Manager (Ctrl + Shift + Esc), navigate to the Startup Apps tab, find PredatorSense, and click Disable.

03

Launch Predator Control

Right-click the downloaded PredatorControlApp.exe and choose Run as administrator (necessary for communicating with the WMI hardware layers). The app registers itself on startup automatically.

System Requirements

Hardware

Acer Predator laptop supporting the AcerGamingFunction WMI interface.

OS

Windows 10 or Windows 11 (64-bit builds recommended).

Runtime

.NET 10.0 Runtime or newer is required to execute the application.

Permissions

Must run with Administrator privileges to send hardware commands.
